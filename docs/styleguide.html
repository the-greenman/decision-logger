06 — Minimum Viable Decision (MVD) Card
MVD-007 We will use consent, not consensus, for operational decisions active
What was decided
Operational decisions pass when no-one has a paramount objection. Consent does not require enthusiasm, only the absence of a blocking concern.
Why
Consensus was producing gridlock on minor items. The group agreed that requiring agreement from everyone on everything conflates strategy with administration.
What wasn't chosen
Simple majority voting (rejected — felt too adversarial, produces winners and losers). Full consensus retained for boundary and purpose decisions.
When to revisit
Annual review, or if a consent decision is later disputed by more than two members. Next: June 2026.
The core artifact. Black header grounds it as a formal record. Mono labels throughout. No colour coding — status is text, not traffic-light.

Design principles
Principle In practice
Cheap office IBM Plex, paper-warm background, no gradients, no shadows, no border-radius. Looks like it was set in Word by someone who knows what they're doing.
No accent colour Hierarchy through weight and mono/sans contrast only. Colour would signal urgency or brand. This is neither.
No transitions Hover states snap, not slide. Documents don't animate.
Mono for meta All IDs, labels, dates, status tags, and system text in IBM Plex Mono. Functional distinction, not decorative.
Text, not icons Status is a word. Actions are words. No chevrons, no checkmarks, no coloured dots.
Immutability as aesthetic Superseded records are struck through, not deleted. The log is a record, not a to-do list.
